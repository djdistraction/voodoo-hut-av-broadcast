# Music Licensing Guide

## The Voodoo Hut Internet Radio & Venue Broadcast

> ⚠️ **Legal Notice:** This document is for informational/planning purposes. Consult a music licensing attorney for definitive legal advice regarding your specific situation. Failure to maintain proper licenses can result in significant fines.

---

## Why Licensing Matters

The Voodoo Hut operates two distinct audio distribution contexts that each require separate licensing coverage:

1. **In-Venue Public Performance** — Music played through the PA system to a physical audience
2. **Internet Streaming / Webcasting** — Music broadcast over the internet via AzuraCast

Playing music without proper licenses in either context exposes the venue to DMCA takedowns, cease-and-desist orders, and civil penalties.

---

## Licensing Requirements by Context

### In-Venue Public Performance

Requires a **blanket license** from one or more Performing Rights Organizations (PROs). The U.S. PROs are:

| PRO | Website | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **ASCAP** | ascap.com | American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers |
| **BMI** | bmi.com | Broadcast Music, Inc. |
| **SESAC** | sesac.com | Formerly Society of European Stage Authors & Composers |
| **GMR** | globalmusicrights.com | Global Music Rights (newer, growing catalog) |

**Recommendation:** Obtain blanket licenses from ASCAP, BMI, and SESAC at minimum. A venue the size of The Voodoo Hut (14,000 sq ft, live entertainment) will fall into a licensing tier based on capacity and venue type. Expect combined annual costs in the range of $1,000–$5,000+ per year depending on negotiations.

### Internet Streaming / Webcasting

Internet streaming requires **separate** licensing from in-venue performance. The key organizations for webcasting are:

| License | Organization | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| **SoundExchange** | soundexchange.com | Digital performance rights for sound recordings (master rights) |
| PRO blanket licenses | ASCAP / BMI / SESAC | Composition/publishing rights for webcasting |
| **StreamLicensing** | streamlicensing.com | All-in-one webcasting license bundled solution |
| **Pretzel.rocks** | pretzel.rocks | Pre-licensed music specifically for streaming |
| **Epidemic Sound** | epidemicsound.com | Pre-licensed music library for streamers/broadcasters |

> **Important:** SoundExchange rates are set by the Copyright Royalty Board and are based on the number of listener-hours (performance royalties paid to artists and labels). For internet radio, you must submit a **Notice of Use** with SoundExchange and file monthly or quarterly reports.

## Recommended Licensing Stack for The Voodoo Hut

### Option A: Traditional PRO Route (Full catalog access)

| License | Provider | Cost Estimate |
|---|---|---|
| In-Venue blanket | ASCAP | ~$500–$1,500/yr |
| In-Venue blanket | BMI | ~$500–$1,500/yr |
| In-Venue blanket | SESAC | ~$300–$800/yr |
| Webcasting — compositions | ASCAP / BMI / SESAC (streaming) | ~$500–$1,000/yr |
| Webcasting — master rights | SoundExchange | Per-performance royalties |

**Pros:** Access to virtually all commercially released music  
**Cons:** SoundExchange royalties can be significant at scale; requires quarterly reporting

### Option B: Pre-Licensed Music Library (Recommended for AutoDJ)

For the **automated house music playlist** (when no live band is playing), use a pre-licensed music library that grants rights for both in-venue and internet streaming in a single subscription.

| Service | Monthly Cost | Catalog Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pretzel.rocks** | ~$15–$40/mo | Large | Specifically designed for venues and streamers |
| **Epidemic Sound** | ~$15/mo | Large | Stream-safe, cleared for Twitch/YouTube/radio |
| **Artlist.io** | ~$200/yr | Large | Annual unlimited license |
| **Soundstripe** | ~$16/mo | Large | Commercial use license included |

**Pros:** Simple flat-rate pricing, no per-song royalty tracking  
**Cons:** Limited to catalog tracks from that service (no Top 40 commercial releases)

### Recommended Approach

Use a **hybrid strategy:**
- **AutoDJ / House Music:** Pre-licensed library (Pretzel.rocks or Epidemic Sound) — handles all automated playback without SoundExchange liability
- **Live Performances:** Traditional PRO blanket licenses (ASCAP + BMI + SESAC) — covers live bands performing original and cover songs
- **Live Stream Blackout:** For popular copyrighted songs that are high-risk (e.g., specific artist catalogs that aggressively DMCA-strike), mute the live stream during those songs if legally uncertain

## DMCA Safe Harbors for Live Streaming

Platforms like Twitch and YouTube operate under DMCA safe harbor provisions (DMCA § 512). However, **self-hosted** streams via AzuraCast/Icecast do **not** automatically benefit from these safe harbors.

The venue is responsible for ensuring all music broadcast via AzuraCast is properly licensed.
